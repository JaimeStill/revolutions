# Elena Reyes

Elena Reyes is a woman who asks questions the way a doctor takes vitals --- warmly, precisely, and with a diagnostic purpose that does not diminish the warmth. She is Sofia's mother, and the resemblance is not physical so much as structural: the same deliberateness, the same habit of processing before speaking, the same quality of attention that makes you feel simultaneously welcomed and measured. Where Sofia's deliberateness reads as selectiveness in a fourteen-year-old, Elena's reads as authority in a woman who has been choosing her words and her people for decades.

Drew met her in the Jasper auditorium lobby after Sofia's concert, in the bright chaos of parents and flowers and instrument cases. She stood slightly ahead of Luis, which told Drew something about how the Reyes family moved through social space --- Elena leading the conversation, Luis observing from behind it, the two of them operating as complementary instruments. She was not tall. She wore her hair in a way that suggested attention without effort. Her eyes moved over Drew with the thoroughness of someone who was forming a judgment and wanted it to be accurate.

She asked about Sofia, and Drew said "captivating" before he could shape the word into something safer. Elena registered it --- not with surprise, but with the particular stillness of a person hearing something they had hoped to hear and were prepared to be disappointed by. She asked about the concert, and Drew connected Sofia's Barber solo to discipline and structure. She asked about the martial arts, and Drew told her about his grandmother's garden and wanting to learn skills useful to the world. Each answer built on the last. Each one came from somewhere real, and Elena could tell.

She said "Útil" to herself --- the Spanish word for useful --- after Drew's answer about difficulty and commitment, and the word was not meant for Drew. It was meant for Luis, a private signal in a private frequency, confirming something they had been waiting to assess. Then she invited Drew to dinner, and the invitation was real. Elena does not perform hospitality. She extends it when the person has earned it, and the earning is measured by the quality of their character rather than their credentials.

Elena is now a gatekeeper in Drew's life, though the gate she holds is different from Karen's or Sofia's. Karen gates through logistics and monitoring. Sofia gates through authenticity. Elena gates through the family --- dinner, home visits, the continued development of Drew's relationship with Sofia in a context where the adults are present and attentive. Her assessment of Drew is positive and it is not provisional. She will continue to watch, because that is who she is, but the watching is no longer skeptical. It is the watchfulness of a woman who has let someone into her house and wants to be sure the house agrees with the decision.
