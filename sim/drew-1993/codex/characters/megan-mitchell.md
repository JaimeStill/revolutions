# Megan Mitchell

Megan is seventeen now, a senior at Plano Senior High, and college applications have consumed whatever bandwidth her adolescence had not already claimed. She was the first-grader earning gold stars while Drew was learning to walk into preschool without crying. She set the template for achievement in the Mitchell household without meaning to, and Drew absorbed it without her noticing.

The three-year age gap and the temperamental difference between them mean they exist in parallel more than they interact. Megan's emotional life is a weather system Drew observes from across the house --- the boyfriend drama, the tearful phone calls, the slammed bedroom door, the reconciliation, the next crisis. Drew watches this the way he watches everything: systematically, looking for patterns. He is not conscious of what he is doing. He is building a dataset about what happens when emotions are not managed, when the inhibition that comes so naturally to him is absent. It frightens him slightly, not Megan herself but the possibility that this kind of volatility exists inside everyone and that his own composure is a choice rather than a nature.

Megan barely registers Drew's interior life. He is her younger brother, quiet and undemanding, the easy one. She is about to leave for college, and when she does, Drew will become the only child in a house where the easy child has always been the invisible one. Neither of them has reckoned with what that will mean.
