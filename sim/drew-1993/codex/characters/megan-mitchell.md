# Megan Mitchell

Megan is fourteen, a freshman at Plano Senior High, and already pulling away from the family with the centrifugal force of adolescence. She plays varsity soccer. She was the first-grader earning gold stars while Drew was learning to walk into preschool without crying. She set the template for achievement in the Mitchell household without meaning to, and Drew absorbed it without her noticing.

Drew admires her. He watches her move through the house with the easy confidence of someone who has already figured out the social world he is about to enter. She is, to him, evidence that competence translates --- that being good at things earns you passage. But Megan barely sees Drew. He is her younger brother, quiet and undemanding, part of the furniture of a home she is in the process of outgrowing. She is not cruel about it. She is simply elsewhere. Her invisibility to him is not malice; it is the ordinary narcissism of being fourteen, and it stings in the ordinary way.
