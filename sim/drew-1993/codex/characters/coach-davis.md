# Coach Davis

Coach Davis runs the Jasper Middle School wrestling program the way former military men run things in Texas public schools --- whistle, sprint, whistle, shoot, whistle, sprawl. Practice is structured and relentless. He does not explain himself. He expects effort, and effort is the primary currency of the relationship.

Drew joined the wrestling team in seventh grade, already carrying three years of jiu-jitsu ground work and muay thai hip movement. Coach Davis noticed within two weeks. The other seventh-graders had to be taught to sprawl by drill; Drew's hips moved instinctively, the result of hundreds of hours on Tran's mats. Coach Davis began using Drew for demonstrations --- "Mitchell, show them the hip escape" --- which carried its own social currency in the wrestling room's hierarchy.

The relationship is institutional in nature. Coach Davis respects Drew's work ethic and cross-training advantage. He remembers effort year to year and rewards it with increased responsibility and trust. Outside of the wrestling room, they do not interact. But within it, Coach Davis is another adult who speaks Drew's language: competence demonstrated, competence recognized, nothing more said than needs to be.
