# Greg Mitchell

Greg Mitchell is an engineer at Alcatel, deep in the Plano Telecom Corridor --- a man who speaks in solutions rather than feelings. He survived the 2000 restructuring that took three men on his team, came home carrying a cardboard box of their desk photos, and went back to work the next Monday. He was promoted in 2003. He drives to Oklahoma for disaster relief and says things like "This changes everything" only when he means it.

His relationship with Drew is built on a currency of competence. Greg does not say he is proud of his son; he hands him a wrench. When Drew started mowing the lawn without being asked, Greg responded by trusting him with the sprinkler timer, then the oil check on the Camry, then the loose hinge on the garage door. Each task was a small transfer of authority, and Drew understood every one.

The martial arts opened a new channel. Greg attended one of Drew's tournaments --- sat in the bleachers without cheering loudly, arms folded across his chest, simply present. Afterward, in the truck, Drew gave a technical debrief: the scoring, the strategy, where he'd been outmatched. Greg asked questions with the attentiveness he gave engineering problems. It was the most sustained conversation they'd had in months, and it happened because Drew had given Greg something Greg knew how to respond to: a domain with rules, a performance that could be analyzed, a son who was competent in terms Greg could see and measure. Now Greg asks about training, and Drew answers with more than "fine."

They still do not talk about fear or sadness. They talk about technique and mower blades and the computer projects Drew is building with Marcus. The channel between them is wider than it was --- Megan's college applications have consumed Karen's bandwidth, and Greg's trust in Drew is at its highest point. But the channel is still shaped by what both of them are willing to say, and neither of them has tested its edges.
