# Carol Novak

Carol Novak is Karen's mother and the most important intellectual relationship in Drew's life. She started picking him up from school in 2000, when Greg's restructuring and Karen's extra shifts left a gap in the afternoons, and what began as childcare became something richer.

Nana Carol's house operates on different rules. She gives Drew books --- not children's books, but whatever is on her shelves --- and lets him talk about them. She taught him to garden: to identify herbs by smell, to repot seedlings at the right moment, to read soil the way Greg reads a wiring diagram. He fixed her back porch railing with wood glue and a C-clamp, and she thanked him the way you thank a colleague, not a child. In her garage he built a working pulley from clothesline and a paint can, and when he explained the mechanics, she listened as if his explanation mattered. She is the adult who treats his mind as something to feed rather than manage, and in her backyard, elbow-deep in potting soil, Drew is the closest he gets to being unselfconscious. She is giving him something no one else in his life provides: the experience of being seen for what he thinks, not what he does.
