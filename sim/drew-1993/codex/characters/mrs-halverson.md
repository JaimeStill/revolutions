# Mrs. Halverson

Mrs. Halverson was Drew's sixth-grade homeroom teacher at Jasper Middle School --- a short woman who wore reading glasses on a beaded chain and kept a jar of peppermints on her desk that she never explicitly offered but never moved out of reach. She called everyone "friend" and meant it in a way that made the word feel like an actual offer rather than the rote warmth of an institution.

Her homeroom, Room 114, was the anchor of Drew's first year at Jasper. The fluorescent lights hummed. The peppermints were always there. Mrs. Halverson represented something Drew needed in the disorienting transition from elementary school: a room with calm rules that did not change. Drew relaxed in her presence by a degree that was visible only if you knew what his baseline tension looked like.

She is no longer Drew's teacher by eighth grade, but they still cross paths in the hallway. She calls him "friend." He smiles back and means it. The relationship has completed its formal arc, but the memory of her room persists as a reference point --- proof that institutional spaces can be made warm by the specific person who fills them.
