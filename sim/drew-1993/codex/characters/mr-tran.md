# Mr. Tran

Mr. Tran runs a martial arts gym off Parker Road in a strip mall between a nail salon and a check-cashing place. No banners, no trophy cases in the lobby, no children's birthday party packages. Just mats, heavy bags, and a back office where he does paperwork between classes. The gym smells of vinyl, sweat, and the eucalyptus oil he uses on sore muscles.

He is a compact man in his fifties who fought professionally in Thailand and communicates in corrections rather than praise. He shook Drew's hand on the first day and said, "You're small. That's fine. Small means fast if you work." This was the entire onboarding process.

Drew started training at Tran's in the summer of 2004, at eleven. Three years later, the relationship has deepened from instructor-student to something closer to mentor-apprentice, though Mr. Tran would not use that word. He pushed Drew into competition before Drew felt ready, adjusted training when Drew joined the school wrestling team, and recognized Drew's cross-training instincts as an asset rather than a distraction. His approval is expressed structurally: he began letting Drew help teach the younger kids' fundamentals class, which is a mark of respect in the gym's hierarchy that carries more weight than any compliment.

Mr. Tran offers life advice disguised as fighting advice. "You don't control the other person. You control the space. The space does the rest." Drew has heard variations of this principle applied to distance management, to tournament strategy, and to a conversation about a kid at school who was giving Drew trouble. The advice was identical each time. Mr. Tran does not distinguish between the domains.

He is the third adult authority figure in Drew's life --- after Greg and Carol --- who communicates through a language of demonstrated competence, and the first who communicates through Drew's body rather than his mind. The honest feedback of physical training, the gap between knowing and doing that cannot be intellectualized past, is something Mr. Tran embodies without explaining. He does not teach philosophy. He teaches technique, and the philosophy is in the technique.
