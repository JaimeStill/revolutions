# Kyle Briggs

Kyle Briggs was the fifth-grade alpha at Mendenhall --- the boy who established hierarchy by finding out who flinched. He tested Drew twice. Once with words, once with a shove. Drew did not flinch either time. He did not fight back, either. He simply stood there and waited, offering nothing --- no fear, no anger, no reaction Kyle could use. Kyle, finding no purchase, moved on to softer targets.

It was not bravery, exactly. It was something closer to calculation --- Drew's instinct that reacting would cost more than absorbing. The encounters ended well enough, but they planted a seed. Drew could feel the gap between what had happened and what might have happened if Kyle had been less bored or more committed. That gap was one of the reasons Drew sought out *The Art of War* on Nana Carol's bookshelf the summer before middle school, and one of the reasons he walked into Tran's Martial Arts and started training. Kyle Briggs was a problem Drew had survived, not solved, and Drew does not leave problems unsolved if he can help it.

By eighth grade, Kyle is bigger --- football team, weight room, part of the athlete faction that dominates the social hierarchy. He and Drew have had no direct conflict since elementary school. But Kyle is part of the power structure that eighth grade is about to sharpen, and the ecosystem is smaller now, more pressurized. Everyone is more visible to everyone.
