# Connor Huang

Connor Huang was Drew's first real friend --- the kid who showed up in kindergarten at Mendenhall and didn't need Drew to talk much. They built Lego spaceships on Saturdays, the kind of parallel play that works perfectly at five and six because neither boy has yet learned to perform friendship for an audience.

The drift completed sometime in seventh grade without a fight or a conversation. Connor found the skater table in the Jasper cafeteria within the first month of sixth grade, pulled there by a gravity Drew could see but did not share. Skateboarding, video games, the social optics of who was watching and who approved --- Connor had arrived at an awareness of performance that Drew either had not reached or had decided was not worth the energy. By eighth grade they are friendly without being friends. They nod in the hallway. They know of each other's lives through the school grapevine, not through each other. The Lego spaceships are years behind them, and what replaced them was not a different shared activity but the quiet recognition that a childhood friendship had served its purpose and ended without anyone being wrong.
