# Connor Huang

Connor Huang was Drew's first real friend --- the kid who showed up in kindergarten at Mendenhall and didn't need Drew to talk much. They built Lego spaceships on Saturdays, the kind of parallel play that works perfectly at five and six because neither boy has yet learned to perform friendship for an audience.

But Connor is changing. He has discovered skateboarding and video games, and more importantly, he has begun to care about social optics --- who is watching, who thinks what, where you stand in the invisible hierarchy that organizes fifth-grade boys. Drew has not arrived at that awareness, or has decided it is not worth the energy. The result is a friendship that is drifting without rupture, two boys moving at different speeds toward different versions of what it means to be twelve. Neither has the vocabulary to name what is happening. They still sit together at lunch, but the Lego spaceships are years behind them, and nothing has replaced them yet.
