# Scene: Early October 2007 — After the Saturday at Carol's

**Year:** 2007 | **Age:** 14 | **Season:** Early fall

## What Happened

The courtyard lunches have been running for two to three weeks. Most days, 12:15, the bench by the courtyard doors. The conversations have moved past the getting-to-know-you layer into something natural yet intense — Sofia's dry humor, Drew's structural thinking, the way they keep finding new dimensions in each other's perspectives. Neither drops other commitments for it. Sofia still has orchestra. Drew still has the gym three times a week and Saturdays with Marcus. The connection grows in the spaces between everything else.

On a Saturday in early October, Drew brought Sofia into his conversation with Nana Carol. Not as an announcement — it came out sideways, the way things come out with Carol. He told her everything: the letter, the courtyard lunches, the fear, the wanting. When Carol asked what he'd been about to say when the filter caught "I want" in the courtyard, Drew said the full sentence: "I want to do this again every day for as long as you will have me." First time he's said it complete, to anyone.

Carol received it the way Carol receives things — without making it smaller or larger. She told him the gap between knowing something and feeling settled about it is most of being a person. She told him to let Sofia see the real version when the moment's right. Drew thanked her. The conversation closed naturally and the Saturday stayed a Saturday.

## Psychological State

The emotional inhibition schema is weaker than it's ever been. The courtyard "I want" was desire outrunning the filter. The Carol conversation was structurally different — the filter simply wasn't there. Drew expressed a naked relational desire calmly, in full, and had it received without consequence. He now has an internal model for what expression without suppression feels like.

Carol's reframe — "you don't grow out of that gap, you get better at living inside it" — gave Drew a new cognitive tool. His default is to build frameworks that resolve things. This framework says the discomfort is workable, not solvable. He's carrying that.

Three data points in the pattern now: the letter, the courtyard, Carol's kitchen. It's becoming a sequence, not an exception.

## Active Threads

- **Sofia — courtyard rhythm established**: 2-3 weeks of daily lunches. The connection is deepening — natural yet intense. Drew is staying grounded, being himself, letting Sofia form her own opinions. The next significant threshold is whether Drew can let Sofia see the unfiltered version — the thing Carol told him to do — in a higher-stakes context where the filter would normally engage.
- **Carol now holds the full picture**: She knows about Sofia — the letter, the lunches, the wanting. She is the second person (after Marcus) in Drew's trust hierarchy who holds this. She would not insert herself unless asked, but she is now a resource.
- **Marcus informed, friendship absorbing it**: Marcus has known since day two. The friendship took it cleanly. Emotional content has entered their channel. The two-world balance (Marcus and Sofia, building and feeling) is currently clean — no scheduling conflict, no emotional tension. The test is whether that holds.
- **Karen still doesn't know**: Sofia does not exist in Karen's awareness. Carol knows, Marcus knows, Karen does not. Drew is managing this through selective presentation, not deception. When Karen finds out, the easy child becomes more complicated.
- **Honors track visibility**: The courtyard lunches are happening in a small ecosystem. The social reading of Drew is quietly shifting — the drifter now has a specific attachment point. This hasn't created pressure yet, but it will.
- **Tournament season**: Fall competitions at Tran's. Drew's physical outlet continues. The emotional landscape underneath the training has changed but the training itself holds.
- **Identity consolidation approaching**: Three beats of the same pattern — acting on want in the relational domain. The question is whether this strand integrates into a stable self-concept or remains context-dependent. Sofia's response to the "real version" will be load-bearing.
