# Scene: September 2007 — First Day of Eighth Grade, After Third Period

**Year:** 2007 | **Age:** 14 | **Season:** Early fall

## What Happened

First day of eighth grade at Jasper Middle School. Drew arrived with the letter in his backpack. Marcus was at his locker — back from Louisiana, taller, easy rhythm restored instantly. Drew didn't mention the letter.

The morning was reconnaissance. Drew mapped the new schedule, the new social geography. Spotted Kyle Briggs in the hall — bigger, football jersey, moving with a pack. One second of eye contact, no nod.

Third period: Honors English with Mrs. Patterson. Sofia Reyes sat three seats to Drew's left. She looked the same and different — longer hair, a green jacket that shouldn't have worked but did. She opened her notebook before class started, which Drew noticed because he does the same thing.

After class, Drew waited for the room to clear. Three people left. He walked to her desk. "Hey. Sofia, right?" — as if he didn't know. He handed her the folded letter. "I wrote this over the summer. No pressure to do anything with it. Just wanted you to have it." She took it. Looked at it. Looked at him. "Okay," she said. Not cold, not warm. Just receiving. Drew said "See you around" and walked out without looking back. His hands were shaking. He put them in his pockets.

## The Letter

*Sofia,*

*I know we don't really talk, but I've wanted to say something since last year.*

*I think you're someone I'd like to know better. I've noticed that you think about things before you say them, and when you do say something it's usually the smartest thing anyone says all class. That thing you said in Mrs. Kelley's room last spring about how every unreliable narrator thinks they're the reliable one — I went home and thought about that for like two days.*

*I don't know if you'd ever want to hang out or talk. If not, that's okay. But I'd rather say it than not.*

*— Drew*

## Psychological State

The letter is delivered. The shift from Sunday is now irreversible — not the feeling, but the action. Drew chose to want something and act on it. The emotional inhibition schema is still structurally present (letter rather than words, walked away rather than stayed), but its monopoly on emotional expression has been broken. His hands shook. He didn't retreat from the shaking. Self-concept tested in a new domain: not competence but vulnerability.

## Active Threads

- **Sofia's response**: Unknown. The letter is in her hands. Drew gave her space — no expectation of immediate response, no hovering. The next move is hers.
- **Marcus in the dark**: Drew's closest friend doesn't know about the letter or about Sofia. If/when Marcus finds out, it will be the first time Drew has shared emotional rather than intellectual vulnerability with a peer.
- **Eighth grade social dynamics**: The honors track is small. If Sofia tells anyone, the news radius is short. Drew's drifter reputation may be complicated by being known as someone who writes letters to girls.
- **Mrs. Patterson's English class**: Third period is now loaded. Drew and Sofia share a room every day. Whatever happens next happens in proximity.
- **The tournament season**: Fall competitions approaching at Tran's. Drew has the physical outlet, but the emotional landscape has shifted underneath the training.
